General

The ultimate (probably) guide for making your OWN models of trees, plants, and other natural organic stuff! (mostly trees and plants)
While a lot of guides and videos talk about low poly and stylized foliage creation, this guide is aimed at the modeling of realistic and semi-realistic looking vegetation; For film, videogames, etc.
I'm mainly creating this guide to figure out a way to model high detail realistic foliage, then lower their quality for games, etc.

  • http://wiki.polycount.com/wiki/Foliage
    Contains links to articles and discussions, has some info about how foliage textures and its vertex normals work, links for some tools, and examples.
  • The Vegetation of Horizon Zero Dawn
    Presentation talking about how the foliage works; the wind simulation, shading, texture packing, normals, etc. Uses SpeedTree for Modeling; 5 LODs. Doesn't really go into much details for the asset creation part.
  • Modular Vegetation Techniques for Games - Billy Matjiunis
    In-depth walkthrough of a workflow (Uses industry-standard tools)

Preparing Materials

  • Removing the background (for leaves)
    1. AI Background Removal
    • Background Removal w/ transformers.js huggingface space
    • BRIA-RMBG
      The first one can bleed translucency into the main subject, and the second one, seems sharper and bleeds less, but it always depends so try both.
    1. Post-Background-Removal Cleanup with GIMP
    • Open the modified image in GIMP and cleanup the stuff that hasn't been correctly removed.
    • You can use the eraser to manually remove big stuff far from the main subject, then go to Layer->Transparency->Threshold Alpha to get rid of translucency
  • Isolating the bark/stem. (for bark/stem)
    Sometimes you need to get only the texture of the stem/bark, then make it tileable for wider usage.
  • Creating a texture atlas. (for leaves/branches/small stems)
    Combine all the leaf/branch textures you've made in a single texture atlas with a transparent background (You can use GIMP or your software of choice)
    A lot plant/tree models use a single texture atlas for the leaves and branches, so that there is no need to use many different textures files.
  • Conversion to PBR Material.
    For a realistic look and lighting you need to convert your atlas and bark into a PBR texture set.
    You can use Materialize as a free option, or Substance Sampler.
  • Baking modeled branches as a material
